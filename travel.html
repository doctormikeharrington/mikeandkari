Travel

Getting to Winnipeg

By Air

Winnipeg James Armstrong Richardson International Airport (YWG)

Winnipeg's airport is located about 10 km (15-20 minutes) from downtown. It's served by all major Canadian airlines and has connections to many US cities.

Airlines serving Winnipeg: Air Canada, WestJet, Flair Airlines, Swoop, United, Delta, and American Airlines (seasonal).

Getting to Downtown:

  • Taxi/Rideshare: ~$25-35 CAD to downtown
  • Rental cars available at the airport
  • Winnipeg Transit Route 15 connects the airport to downtown
Airport Website

By Car

Winnipeg is accessible by several major highways:

  • From the East (Ontario): Trans-Canada Highway 1
  • From the West (Saskatchewan): Trans-Canada Highway 1 or Highway 16
  • From the South (USA): Interstate 29 to Highway 75 (Emerson border crossing)

Approximate driving times:

  • Regina: 5.5 hours
  • Saskatoon: 8 hours
  • Minneapolis: 7 hours
  • Thunder Bay: 7 hours

Border Crossing: If you're coming from the US, the Emerson border crossing on Highway 75 is the most direct route. Make sure to have your passport and any required documentation.

Getting Around Winnipeg

Both wedding venues are in downtown Winnipeg and are within easy reach of each other.

Walking: Downtown Winnipeg is very walkable. The ceremony and reception venues are about 1 km apart on Broadway.

Taxi & Rideshare: Uber and local taxi services are available throughout the city.

Parking: Street parking and parking garages are available near both venues. The Fort Garry Hotel offers valet parking.

Public Transit: Winnipeg Transit serves the downtown area. Visit winnipegtransit.com for routes and schedules.

While You're in Winnipeg

If you're extending your stay, Winnipeg has plenty to offer! Here are some local favorites:

The Forks

Historic meeting place with markets, restaurants, and beautiful riverfront walks.

Canadian Museum for Human Rights

Stunning architecture and thought-provoking exhibits.

Assiniboine Park & Zoo

Beautiful park with gardens, trails, and the Journey to Churchill exhibit.

Exchange District

Historic neighbourhood with great restaurants, shops, and nightlife.
